About

Hello there, I'm Joan! — also known as "Mum", "Miss", "Bruh!", and "Nurse" — a student of Digital Design and Web Development, transitioning into tech from a career in healthcare. With a Master's degree in Psychology and a deep interest in user experience and accessibility, I'm passionate about creating digital solutions that are thoughtful, inclusive, and purpose-driven.

What excites me most about frontend development is the entire process — receiving a design brief, planning the user journey, designing visually engaging interfaces, and finally bringing them to life with clean, responsive code. My background in healthcare gives me a strong foundation in communication, empathy, and problem-solving — skills I now apply to every project I work on.

I bring a unique blend of creativity and structure: I’m super-organised, thrive under pressure, and I have experience leading teams, maintaining detailed records, and facilitating clear communication. I’m especially skilled at gathering and filtering user requirements to meet specific goals — a vital part of delivering effective UX solutions.

Outside the code editor, I enjoy face painting and diving into books and audiobooks on forensic psychology and forensic science. Fun Fact: I still remember my first phone — a Nokia 5110 — and the first game I ever played: Chuckie Egg on the ZX Spectrum!

I am interested in voluntary tech projects for charities and have future plans to build a health clinic app, create a product database, and learn React. Ultimately, one day, I hope to be a role model and mentor for other women making a career change into tech, helping them realise how the skills they've already gained can shine in a tech environment.
